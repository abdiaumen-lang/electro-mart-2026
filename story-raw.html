From Algiers with Love

It began in a small, sun-drenched kitchen in the Casbah of Algiers. Here, amidst the aroma of roasting cumin and simmering chorba, the seeds of Le 23 were sown. Not in a business plan, but in a grandmother's tattered notebook, filled with recipes passed down like precious heirlooms.

We believe that food is more than sustenance; it is a storyteller. Every tagine we serve carries the whisper of olive groves, the heat of the Sahara, and the gentle breeze of the Mediterranean. Our chefs honor these traditions, using techniques that have remained unchanged for centuries.
